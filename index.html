🚗 Auto Insurance Claims Process

Follow these steps when filing an auto insurance claim in North Carolina

1
🚨

Ensure Safety

Check for injuries and call 911 if needed. Move to a safe location if possible.

2
👮

Call Police

Report the accident to police even for minor crashes. Get the report number.

3
📝

Exchange Information

Get driver info, insurance details, and witness contact information.

4
📸

Document Scene

Take photos of vehicles, damage, scene, and write down what happened.

5
📞

Contact Insurance

Call your insurance company to report the claim as soon as possible.

6
🔍

Work with Adjuster

Schedule inspection and work with adjuster to assess damage and coverage.

📋 NC Minimum Insurance Requirements

North Carolina requires minimum coverage of 30/60/25 (Bodily Injury per person/per accident, Property Damage)

🏠 Home Insurance Claims Process

Step-by-step guide for filing home insurance claims in North Carolina

1
⚠️

Ensure Safety

Make sure everyone is safe. Don't enter damaged areas until cleared by officials.

2
📹

Document Damage

Take photos and videos of all damage before making any temporary repairs.

3
📱

Contact Insurance

Report the claim to your insurance company immediately.

4
🔧

Mitigate Damage

Make reasonable temporary repairs to prevent further damage. Save receipts.

5
👨‍💼

Work with Adjuster

Schedule inspection and provide documentation of losses.

6
🏠

Complete Repairs

Get estimates, complete repairs, and submit receipts for reimbursement.

📜 NC Insurance Laws

North Carolina follows contributory negligence law. Minimum coverage required: 30/60/25.

  • $30,000 bodily injury per person
  • $60,000 bodily injury per accident
  • $25,000 property damage

⏰ Important Deadlines

Key time limits to remember for insurance claims in North Carolina:

  • Report claims promptly (within 24-48 hours)
  • Property damage claims: 3 years statute of limitations
  • Personal injury claims: 3 years statute of limitations

💡 Helpful Tips

Be prepared and know what to do before you need to file a claim

🚗 Keep in Your Car

  • Insurance card and policy information
  • Emergency contact numbers
  • Pen and paper for notes
  • Phone charger
  • First aid kit

📄 Important Documents

  • Current insurance policy
  • Vehicle registration and title
  • Driver's license
  • Home inventory for property claims
  • Receipts for valuable items

📱 Preparation Tips

  • Take photos regularly of your property
  • Know your policy limits and deductibles
  • Have your agent's contact information saved
  • Review coverage annually
  • Consider umbrella insurance
