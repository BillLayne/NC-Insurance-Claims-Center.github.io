🚗 Auto Insurance Claims Process

Follow these steps when filing an auto insurance claim in North Carolina

1

Ensure Safety

Check for injuries and call 911 if needed. Move to a safe location if possible.

2

Call Police

Report the accident to police even for minor crashes. Get the report number.

3

Exchange Information

Get driver info, insurance details, and witness contact information.

4

Document Scene

Take photos of vehicles, damage, scene, and write down what happened.

5

Contact Insurance

Call your insurance company to report the claim as soon as possible.

6

Work with Adjuster

Schedule inspection and work with adjuster to assess damage and coverage.

📋 NC Minimum Insurance Requirements

North Carolina requires minimum coverage of 50/100/50 (Bodily Injury per person/per accident, Property Damage)

🏠 Home Insurance Claims Process

Step-by-step guide for filing home insurance claims in North Carolina

1

Ensure Safety

Make sure everyone is safe. Don't enter damaged areas until cleared by officials.

2

Document Damage

Take photos and videos of all damage before making any temporary repairs.

3

Contact Insurance

Report the claim to your insurance company immediately.

4

Mitigate Damage

Make reasonable temporary repairs to prevent further damage. Save receipts.

5

Work with Adjuster

Schedule inspection and provide documentation of losses.

6

Complete Repairs

Get estimates, complete repairs, and submit receipts for reimbursement.

🚗 Find Your Car's True Value

Know your car's Kelley Blue Book value before filing a total loss claim

Why Knowing Your Car's Value Matters

When your car is totaled, insurance companies offer the Actual Cash Value (ACV) - not the dealer retail price. Use this tool to:

  • ✅ Get your car's official Kelley Blue Book value instantly
  • ✅ Understand the difference between retail and insurance values
  • ✅ Negotiate confidently with solid data
  • ✅ Ensure you receive a fair settlement

🏷️ Dealer Retail Value

The price at a car lot - always higher because it includes:

  • • Dealer profit margin
  • • Reconditioning costs
  • • Sales commissions
  • • Warranties & overhead

⚖️ Insurance ACV

What your car was worth before damage - based on:

  • • Private-party values
  • • Recent sales data
  • • Your car's condition
  • • NO dealer profit

💡 Pro Tip: Insurance pays the Actual Cash Value (ACV), not retail. KBB gives you both values so you understand the full picture and can negotiate effectively.

📜 NC Insurance Laws

North Carolina follows contributory negligence law. Minimum coverage required:

  • • $50,000 bodily injury per person
  • • $100,000 bodily injury per accident
  • • $50,000 property damage

⏰ Important Deadlines

Key time limits to remember:

  • • Report claims promptly (24-48 hours)
  • • Property damage claims: 3 years
  • • Personal injury claims: 3 years
